History of Crochet

Crochet, unlike knitting, uses one hooked tool to pull the yarn through hoops. The timeline of when crochet began aren't clear, but it has been said to have started in Europe. Overtime, it slowly started to grow in popularity. Crochet was established as a household skill by early 20th century. With online tutorials, readily avalible patterns, plenty of books, it has become easier to learn. Nowadays, you are able to find beginner-friendly crochet kits that includes the materials needed, and instructions.

Intro

For many people, crocheting can be meditative. It can reduce anxiety, stress, and cortisol levels. Learning this trade can feel extremely rewarding while also giving you instant gratification. Whether you want to pick up another hobby, make homemade gifts for your loved ones, or are in the need for some one-of-a-kind clothing, anyone can learn crochet. Just like every new hands-on hobby, you might need some guidance. Thankfully, there are plenty of resources to help get you started.

Benefits to Crochet

  • Manage Chronic Pain
  • Cognitive function
  • Enhances hand-eye coordination
  • Offers creative outlet
